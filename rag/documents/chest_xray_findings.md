# Chest X-ray Findings — Patient Guidance / إرشادات نتائج أشعة الصدر

## What a chest X-ray shows
A chest X-ray images the lungs, heart, and chest wall. The AI screens for common
findings and flags them for a radiologist to confirm. It is a screening aid, not
a final diagnosis.

## Common findings explained
- **Consolidation / infiltrate:** a hazy white area where air-filled lung has
  filled with fluid, pus, or cells — often **pneumonia** (a lung infection).
- **Pleural effusion:** fluid collecting between the lung and chest wall; can
  cause breathlessness.
- **Cardiomegaly:** the heart looks enlarged; may relate to blood pressure or
  heart conditions and needs cardiology follow-up.
- **Atelectasis:** a collapsed or partially collapsed area of lung.
- **Pneumothorax:** air in the space around the lung, which can make it collapse
  — this is urgent.
- **Nodule / mass:** a spot that needs further imaging to characterize.

## Why screening matters
Catching pneumonia, effusions, or an enlarged heart early lets treatment start
sooner, when it is most effective. Many findings are treatable when found early.

## Red flags — seek urgent care
Severe shortness of breath, chest pain, blue lips or fingertips, coughing up
blood, or a high fever with difficulty breathing.

## بالعربية (ملخص)
أشعة الصدر تصوّر الرئتين والقلب وجدار الصدر. من أهم النتائج: **التصلّب/الارتشاح**
(غالبًا التهاب رئوي)، **الانصباب الجنبي** (سائل حول الرئة)، **تضخّم القلب**،
و**استرواح الصدر (Pneumothorax)** وهو حالة عاجلة. الفحص المبكر يسمح ببدء العلاج
في وقت مبكر. اطلب رعاية عاجلة عند ضيق تنفّس شديد، ألم بالصدر، ازرقاق الشفاه، أو
سعال مصحوب بدم.

> Imaging findings must be confirmed by a radiologist and correlated with the
> patient's symptoms.
